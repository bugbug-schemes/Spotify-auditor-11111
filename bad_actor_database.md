1\. Scope and Methodology

This database catalogs entities known or strongly suspected of creating, distributing, or facilitating non-authentic content on music streaming platforms, with a specific focus on content that Spotify intentionally presents to listeners as if it were music by real, independent artists.

1.1 What This Database Covers

**Platform-complicit content manufacturing:** Corporate entities that produce bulk music under fabricated artist identities, distributed with platform knowledge and often preferential playlist placement. This is the core focus.

**AI-native artist projects:** Fully AI-generated artists/bands presented as human, such as The Velvet Sundown, which gained over 1 million Spotify listeners before being exposed.

**AI content labels/companies:** Companies like Stage Zero (Timbaland) that explicitly create and market AI-generated music entities.

1.2 What This Database Excludes

**Streaming fraud (Spotify-as-victim):** Bot farms, fake stream manipulation, and royalty theft schemes where external actors defraud the platform (e.g., the Michael Smith \$10M fraud case). These are crimes against the platform, not cases of the platform presenting non-authentic content to users.

**AI impersonation/voice cloning:** Tracks uploaded to real artists\' profiles without consent (e.g., the Emily Portman and Paul Bender cases). This is identity theft, not content manufacturing.

3.3 The \'91 Swedes\' Network

Beyond the named individuals, the Dagens Nyheter investigation identified a much larger network: **91 Swedish individuals hiding behind 5,700 false artist names, responsible for more than 13,000 songs.**

DN\'s analysis of the \'Peaceful Piano\' playlist (7.5M users) found that around 100 of its supposedly independent composers were in fact aliases belonging to this Swedish network. On many instrumental playlists, more than 20% of tracks were by a single producer under multiple fake names.

**Key Pattern:** These producers accept lower royalty rates (reportedly \~25% of standard rates) in exchange for guaranteed playlist placement. The arrangement is lucrative due to the sheer volume of streams that mood/background playlists generate.

10.2 Financial Model

The PFC system\'s economics are fundamentally about reducing Spotify\'s cost per stream:

**Standard royalty:** \$0.003-\$0.005 per stream paid to rights holders through pro-rata streamshare model.

**PFC royalty:** Reportedly \~25% of standard rates. PFC providers accept lower per-stream rates in exchange for guaranteed playlist placement and volume.

**Streamshare displacement:** As PFC streams grow as a percentage of total streams, they dilute the royalty pool available to all other artists. This is the mechanism by which ghost artists reduce payments to real artists even if those real artists\' stream counts remain stable.

12\. Database Maintenance and Future Research

12.1 Known Gaps

The following areas require further investigation to complete this database:

**Unrevealed PFC Providers:** Pelly noted \'at least a dozen\' PFC providers in internal Slack. Only 6 have been publicly named. At least 6+ remain unidentified.

**Complete Fake Artist Name Lists:** DN identified 830 for Firefly and 656 for Rohr, but the full list of 5,700+ names from the Swedish network has not been published. STIM registry cross-referencing could expand this.

**Non-Swedish Operations:** All confirmed PFC providers are Swedish. It is highly likely similar operations exist in other countries but have not been investigated by local press.

**AI Content Volume on Spotify:** Deezer reports \~20% of daily uploads are AI-generated. Spotify has not disclosed equivalent figures. Spotify does not label AI content.

**ISRC Registrant Mapping:** ISRC codes in Spotify metadata contain registrant prefixes. Building a mapping of which ISRC prefixes correspond to known PFC providers would enable automated detection.

12.2 Update Cadence

This database should be updated when: new investigative reporting is published (particularly from MBW, DN, Pelly); new AI-generated artist controversies emerge; Spotify policy changes occur; legal proceedings produce new disclosures; or community research identifies new entities.

12.3 Integration with Analyzer Tool

The label/copyright/songwriter watchlists in Section 9 are designed to be directly importable into the analyzer tool as lookup tables. When an analyzed artist\'s metadata matches any entry in these watchlists, the tool should flag the match and adjust the authenticity score accordingly.
