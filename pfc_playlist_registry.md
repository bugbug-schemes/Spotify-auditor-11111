1\. About This Document

This registry catalogs every Spotify editorial playlist that has been publicly identified as containing **Perfect Fit Content (PFC)** --- commissioned tracks produced by production companies and distributed under fabricated artist identities, then given preferential placement on Spotify's own curated playlists. PFC tracks are cheaper for Spotify to host than licensed music from independent or major-label artists, effectively displacing real musicians from playlist positions and reducing their share of the royalty pool.

This document serves as a reference for the Spotify Playlist Authenticity Analyzer tool, providing a known-PFC playlist watchlist against which any user-submitted playlist can be compared. It also provides a foundation for further investigation: the 76 playlists named here represent roughly one-quarter to one-third of the estimated total, based on internal reporting that \"several hundred\" playlists were under PFC team management by 2023.

10\. Recommendations: Uncovering the Remaining Playlists

The 76 playlists documented here represent only a fraction of the estimated total. Pelly reported that "several hundred" playlists were being monitored by Spotify's PFC team (Strategic Programming / "StraP") by 2023, with 150+ described as "nearly entirely" PFC. The following strategies can help identify the rest:

10.1 Spotify API Playlist Scraping

**Approach:** Use the Spotify Web API (or a scraping tool like spotifyscraper) to pull the full track list of every Spotify editorial playlist in the targeted activity categories (sleep, focus, chill, jazz, classical, ambient, lo-fi). For each track, extract the artist name, label, copyright holder, and available metadata.

**What to look for:** Cross-reference every artist against the known fake artist name list and the known PFC provider labels (Epidemic Sound, Firefly Entertainment, Queenstreet/Audiowell, Hush Hush LLC, Catfarm Music AB, Industria Works/Mood Works, Catfish Music Group, Chillmi). Flag any artist that appears on Spotify but not on Deezer, Apple Music, or Tidal --- a strong PFC indicator since many early PFC tracks were Spotify-exclusive.

10.2 Cross-Platform Absence Detection

**Approach:** For any suspect artist, search for them on Deezer, Apple Music, YouTube Music, and Tidal. PFC artists from the early period (2015--2019) were often Spotify-exclusive. While newer PFC may appear on multiple platforms, checking MusicBrainz for metadata richness, Discogs for physical releases, and Last.fm for organic scrobble history can still distinguish real from fabricated.

10.3 STIM/BMI/ASCAP Credit Network Analysis

**Approach:** Dagens Nyheter's breakthrough came from cross-referencing Spotify streaming data with STIM (Swedish copyright society) records. The same approach can be applied via BMI and ASCAP's public repertoire searches. If dozens of "different" artists all share the same songwriter credits, that's a PFC signature. The Genius API can supplement this by revealing songwriter credit networks.

10.4 Playlist History Analysis via Chartmetric

**Approach:** Chartmetric tracks playlist changes over time. Turner and Hu used this to identify the February 2017 mass swap on Ambient Chill where 16 real artists were replaced by 28 Epidemic Sound aliases. Looking for sudden, bulk artist replacements on editorial playlists is a strong signal of PFC deployment. SpotOnTrack is another analytics tool for this purpose.

10.5 Label Clustering Detection

**Approach:** When a single playlist is dominated by tracks from a small number of labels (especially labels with no web presence, no roster page, or shell-company characteristics), that's suspicious. Build a label-concentration score for each playlist: if 3 labels account for \>50% of tracks, investigate further. This is a core feature of the Spotify Playlist Authenticity Analyzer.

10.6 Listener-to-Follower Ratio Anomalies

**Approach:** Turner's original methodology flagged artists with extreme ratios --- e.g., 400,000 monthly listeners but only 898 followers. Real artists build followers through engagement; PFC artists accumulate passive listeners from playlist placement without building any fan relationship. This ratio is available via the Spotify API and is one of the strongest automated signals.

10.7 AI-Generated Profile Image Detection

**Approach:** Multiple sources (Gigazine, Chartmetric) note that PFC artist profile images are frequently AI-generated. Tools like Hive Moderation or Illuminarty can detect AI-generated images. While not conclusive on its own, an AI profile image combined with other PFC signals (no web presence, single-label affiliation, extreme listener/follower ratio) creates a strong composite indicator.

10.8 Community and Crowdsourced Intelligence

**Approach:** The music community has been actively identifying suspect artists since 2016. Reddit threads (r/spotify, r/WeAreTheMusicMakers), music journalist coverage, and tools like the "Is It Fake?" browser extensions can surface leads. Ted Gioia's Honest Broker newsletter has been tracking this story since 2022 and publishes specific examples. Aggregating community reports into a structured database would extend our coverage significantly.

10.9 Freedom of Information / Regulatory Filings

**Approach:** The UK's Competition and Markets Authority (CMA) investigated music streaming and found that licensing agreements contain playlist composition obligations. Swedish corporate filings (Allabolag.se) reveal revenue figures for PFC providers like Queenstreet/Audiowell (SEK 331M in 2023) and Firefly Entertainment. EU transparency requirements and ongoing regulatory interest in streaming economics may yield additional data. The US Copyright Royalty Board proceedings also contain relevant testimony.

11\. Integration with the Playlist Authenticity Analyzer

This registry feeds directly into the analyzer tool in two ways:

**Playlist-Level Match:** If a user submits a playlist URL that matches any playlist in this registry, the tool immediately flags it with the appropriate evidence tier and provides context about the extent of PFC contamination documented for that playlist.

**Artist-Level Cross-Reference:** When analyzing any playlist (whether in this registry or not), the tool checks every artist against the fake artist name database, the PFC provider label watchlist, and the songwriter credit network. This catches PFC content that has migrated to non-registry playlists or new playlists created since this document was compiled.

The tool should also implement the detection strategies outlined in Section 10 as automated signals, particularly the cross-platform absence check (10.2), label clustering detection (10.5), and listener-to-follower ratio analysis (10.6), which can be performed programmatically via the Spotify, Deezer, and MusicBrainz APIs.

12\. Known Gaps & Limitations

**Scale gap:** 76 playlists named vs. "several hundred" reported internally. We are missing 60--75% of the total PFC playlist universe.

**Temporal gap:** The Turner/Hu data is from 2018. Playlists may have been renamed, merged, split, or retired. The Pelly data reflects 2023 internal records. Current (2026) playlist composition may differ.

**Non-Swedish operations:** All major reporting has focused on Swedish PFC providers. Pelly notes that PFC providers operate "outside the US" (plural countries), and that at least a dozen providers exist beyond the six named in Slack messages. Non-Swedish operations are almost entirely undocumented.

**Genre expansion:** PFC was initially concentrated in ambient, jazz, classical, and lo-fi. One source warned it would spread beyond these genres. Whether PFC has expanded into pop, hip-hop, or other high-value categories is unknown from public reporting.

**AI acceleration:** Former Spotify employees have noted that AI could take over PFC production. Whether Spotify has begun using AI-generated PFC (as opposed to human-produced PFC) is undocumented but probable given Ek's 2023 statements about AI content being "a lucrative opportunity" and Epidemic Sound's stated interest in AI generation.
