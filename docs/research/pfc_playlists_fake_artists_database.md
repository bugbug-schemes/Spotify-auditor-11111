Overview & Purpose

This document compiles all publicly documented fake/ghost artist names and PFC-affected playlists from investigative journalism and industry research. It serves as the master reference for the Spotify Playlist Authenticity Analyzer tool, providing verified detection targets for automated analysis.

**Primary Sources:** Music Business Worldwide (MBW) original 50 fake artists list (July 2017); Rolling Stone 2019 follow-up with updated stream counts; Penny Fractions expanded list of 140+ names with 50+ affected playlists (July 2018); Dagens Nyheter investigation identifying 91 Swedish producers behind 5,700 fake names (March 2024); Liz Pelly / Harper\'s Magazine PFC investigation (December 2024).

**Scale Summary:** The MBW original 50 accumulated 2.85 billion streams by 2019. Johan Rohr alone accounts for 15 billion streams under 656 pseudonyms. The full network of 91 Swedish producers uses 5,700 fake names across 13,000+ songs. By 2023, over 150 Spotify playlists were nearly 100% PFC content.

The Broader Swedish Network

**DN found that 91 Swedish individuals are hiding behind 5,700 false artist names, responsible for more than 13,000 songs.** The Peaceful Piano playlist alone (7.5M followers by 2024) included melodies from approximately 100 completely unknown composers that journalists demonstrated were aliases belonging to these Swedish producers.

**Songwriter Credits Trail:** MBW readers traced ISRC codes and searched BMI/ASCAP databases. Tracks by Deep Watch, Piotr Miteska, Karin Borg, and Evolution Of Stars were all credited to Andreas Romdhane and Josef Svedlund, the founders of Queenstreet Content AB (now Audiowell Group). This BMI/ASCAP database cross-reference method is a key detection technique for the analyzer tool.

Detection Patterns for Analyzer Tool

Based on the documented evidence, the following patterns should be implemented as detection heuristics in the analyzer tool.

Artist-Level Red Flags

-   High monthly listeners but extremely low follower count (e.g., 400K listeners / 898 followers)

-   Music appears only on Spotify with zero presence on Apple Music, YouTube Music, Deezer, or Tidal

-   Zero social media presence (no Facebook, Twitter/X, Instagram, SoundCloud)

-   No concert history, no tour dates, no Setlist.fm presence

-   Only a handful of tracks uploaded, all achieving prominent playlist placement

-   No results on Google beyond Spotify profile and auto-generated YouTube rips

-   Artist name appears ethnically diverse but all tracks share identical production style

-   ISRC codes trace to Epidemic Sound, Overtone Studios, Queenstreet/Audiowell, or other known PFC providers

-   BMI/ASCAP credits reveal Romdhane/Svedlund, Johan Rohr, or Christer Sandelin as underlying songwriter

-   Label credits include: Epidemic Sound, Overtone Studios, Firefly Entertainment, Queenstreet Content AB, Catfarm Music AB, Hush Hush LLC, Industria Works, Mood Works, Myndstream

-   No physical releases on Discogs (vinyl, CD)

-   Last.fm scrobble-to-stream ratio near zero (bot streams don\'t scrobble)

-   No songwriter/producer credits on Genius

Playlist-Level Red Flags

-   High percentage of artists matching the fake artist master list in this document

-   Sudden bulk artist swaps (e.g., 16 real artists replaced by 28 unknowns in one update)

-   Disproportionate concentration of tracks from a single distributor (e.g., 65% Epidemic Sound)

-   Genre is mood/activity-based: ambient, sleep, focus, jazz, classical, lo-fi, piano, meditation, yoga

-   Playlist is Spotify first-party (curated by \'Spotify\' editorial team, not user-generated)

-   Artists on playlist have no cross-playlist presence outside mood/activity categories
